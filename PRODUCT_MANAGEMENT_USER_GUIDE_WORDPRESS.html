üí∞ PRICING - How to Change Prices

Increase, decrease, or round your product prices

What Can You Do?

  • Increase prices by a percentage (example: raise all prices by 10%)
  • Decrease prices by a percentage (example: discount everything by 15%)
  • Round prices to neat numbers (example: $19.99 becomes $20.00)
  • Set compare-at prices (the "was $50, now $40" strikethrough price)
  • Update cost per item (what YOU paid for it - for profit tracking)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

A. Increase Price by Percentage
  1. Click the "Increase" button
  2. Enter a number in the "Percentage" box (example: type 10 for 10% increase)
  3. Example: If a product costs $20, a 10% increase makes it $22
  4. Click "Apply Price Changes" button
B. Decrease Price by Percentage
  1. Click the "Decrease" button
  2. Enter a number in the "Percentage" box (example: type 15 for 15% off)
  3. Example: If a product costs $20, a 15% decrease makes it $17
  4. Click "Apply Price Changes" button
C. Round Prices
  1. Click the "Round" button
  2. Choose how to round:
    • Up - $19.50 becomes $20.00
    • Down - $19.50 becomes $19.00
    • Nearest - $19.50 becomes $20.00, but $19.40 becomes $19.00
  3. Choose what to round to:
    • Dollar - Makes prices like $20.00 (no cents)
    • 50 cents - Makes prices end in .00 or .50
    • 99 cents - Makes prices end in .99 ($19.99, $20.99)
  4. Check "Also round compare-at prices" if you want to round sale prices too
  5. Click "Apply Price Changes" button

‚úÖ You'll see: "Successfully updated X products!"

üìÅ COLLECTIONS - Organize Products

Add or remove products from collections

What Can You Do?

  • Add products to one or more collections
  • Remove products from collections
Add to Collections
  1. Click on the dropdown menu to see all your store's collections
  2. Select one or MORE collections (they'll show a checkmark)
  3. Click "Add to Collections" button
  4. Example: Select "Summer Sale" ‚Üí All your products now appear in Summer Sale!
Remove from Collections
  1. Select the collection(s) you want to remove products from
  2. Click "Remove from Collections" button
  3. Example: Season ended? Remove all products from "Summer Sale"

üè∑Ô∏è TAGS - Label Your Products

Add, remove, or replace product tags

What Are Tags?

Tags are keywords that help you organize products.

Examples: "new", "sale", "bestseller", "winter", "featured"

Add Tags (Keeps Existing Tags)
  1. Click the "Add" button
  2. Type your tags, separated by commas
  3. Example: new, featured, bestseller
  4. This ADDS these tags without removing old ones
  5. Click "Update Product Tags"
Remove Tags
  1. Click the "Remove" button
  2. Type the tags you want to DELETE, separated by commas
  3. Example: old, discontinued
  4. This removes only those specific tags (keeps all others)
  5. Click "Update Product Tags"
Replace ALL Tags

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: This DELETES all old tags and replaces with new ones!

  1. Click the "Replace" button
  2. Type your NEW tags
  3. Example: new, spring, 2025
  4. Click "Update Product Tags"

‚úèÔ∏è CONTENT - Edit Titles & Descriptions

Result: "Blue Shirt" becomes "Blue Premium Shirt"

Tip: Leave "Replace With" empty to DELETE the found text!

Part 2: DESCRIPTIONS

Add Prefix or Suffix

Add text before or after existing descriptions (keeps current description)

Example: Replace all "2024" with "2025"

üì¶ INVENTORY - Update Stock Levels

Manage stock quantities, SKU, and barcodes

What Can You Do?

  • Set exact stock numbers (example: set all to 100)
  • Add to current stock (example: add 50 to what's there now)
  • Remove from current stock (example: reduce all by 10)
  • Update SKU (your internal product code)
  • Update Barcode (scannable barcode number)
  • Allow selling when out of stock
Set to Exact Number
  1. Click the "Method" dropdown
  2. Select "Set to"
  3. Enter a number (example: 100)
  4. Result: EVERY variant will have exactly 100 in stock (ignores previous amount)
Add to Existing Stock
  1. Select "Add"
  2. Enter a number (example: 50)
  3. Result: If you had 20 in stock, now you have 70 (20 + 50)
Remove from Existing Stock
  1. Select "Remove"
  2. Enter a number (example: 10)
  3. Result: If you had 20 in stock, now you have 10 (20 - 10)
Optional: Update SKU & Barcode

Update SKU: Check the box and enter your product code (example: SHIRT-BLU-001)

Update Barcode: Check the box and enter barcode (example: 123456789012)
